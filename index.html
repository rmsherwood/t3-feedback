Strong foundation.

Product feedback and working concept designs from a week inside Momentum Express VTF® — submitted with respect by a fellow builder.

From a paying member  ·  Signed up on the strength of JR  ·  Works in product

The core is genuinely strong. JR is an accomplished trader and an excellent teacher — watching him work live is rare and valuable. The mods are great, the community is supportive, and the underlying technology does the right things. The problem is purely presentation and UX: it feels dated, creates unnecessary friction at every key touchpoint, and undersells what's actually a premium product. That's the good news — the hard part is already built.

Priority feedback
Where the funnel is leaking
1
Audio disconnects are an engagement killer
I refreshed 5+ times in a single session. Each time requires re-running through the pre-entry wall of text. This is the single highest-priority issue — not because it's shameful, but because it's the most direct path to killing an otherwise great first impression.
Critical · Fix first
2
Account creation is broken at the top of the funnel
My dad told me about the open house. I couldn't find how to create an account from t3live.com/login. Googled it, landed on portal.t3live.com/users/sign_in — wrong page. Eventually emailed JR directly to get set up. Nobody should have to email the host trader to join. This is killing conversions before they start.
Critical · Top of funnel
3
The chat UI doesn't match user expectations
Users arrive expecting something like Discord or Slack. The current layout — alerts above main chat, tabs for sub-rooms, no visible channel hierarchy — is disorienting. Amber reached out by DM but I struggled to find the conversation again after closing it. The unconventional UX creates friction at every interaction.
UX · High impact
4
Conversion touchpoints are unclear
There's no persistent, obvious path from "I'm enjoying this" to "here's how I become a member." The offer link exists but it's not surfaced at the right moment, in the right context. Open house visitors need a clear next step visible throughout — not just at the end.
Conversion · Revenue impact
5
No intake or follow-up flow for open house visitors
Gathering goals upfront (trading experience, what success looks like, current approach) would let you personalize follow-ups. A post-open-house survey would also give you signal on what's working and who's on the fence. Both are low effort, high yield.
Conversion · Easy win

Growth levers
Content already exists — it just needs polish
Reddit presence is nearly zero
LLMs train on Reddit and most people search it before buying anything. Two posts found — one negative, one organic recommendation. That's the entire public record for a premium trading service. A handful of authentic posts from real members would meaningfully change what shows up when someone googles "T3 Live review."
YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram need a graphics refresh, not more content
The content is already being produced daily and it's genuinely good. The gap is production polish — thumbnails, consistent graphic templates, better titles. Ross Cameron is the benchmark: same raw material, noticeably more effort on presentation. JR's analysis is as good or better. The same clips repurposed for TikTok and Instagram Reels are essentially free distribution once the YouTube workflow is tightened.
